# Kettlebell Hypertrophy Research - 2026-06-12

Goal: add evidence-informed kettlebell workouts for men trying to gain overall muscle. The same hypertrophy principles apply broadly across healthy adults, but the programming below biases toward common male goals: more total lean mass, shoulders/back/chest/arms, legs, and trunk.

## Evidence Summary

- Weekly hard-set volume is a major hypertrophy driver. A dose-response meta-analysis found greater muscle gain with higher weekly resistance-training volume, so the app workouts should repeat major patterns across the week rather than only doing one random circuit.
- Training each muscle at least twice weekly is a practical target. Frequency mainly helps distribute weekly volume and maintain quality.
- Hypertrophy can occur across a wide loading range when sets are performed close to failure. For kettlebells, this means heavy enough bells, slow eccentrics, unilateral work, pauses, and rep targets that end with roughly 0-3 reps in reserve.
- Failure is not mandatory. For safer kettlebell work, use form failure or 1-3 reps in reserve on complex moves, especially swings, cleans, presses, and get-ups.
- Kettlebell-specific research supports swings as a posterior-chain power/strength tool and shows kettlebell swing/snatch/carry patterns load the hip, back, and trunk. For hypertrophy, pair ballistic hinges with slower strength moves such as squats, rows, presses, lunges, floor presses, and carries.

## Programming Rules For The App

- Use 30s work / 30s rest EMOM by default, but prescribe a target effort: finish each work window with about 0-3 clean reps in reserve.
- Prefer 3-5 cycles for hypertrophy workouts. One cycle is not enough volume for muscle growth.
- For unilateral moves, program left and right sides separately.
- Use progression notes: add load first, then reps/quality, then cycles.
- Use placeholders instead of inaccurate exercise illustrations unless there is an exact visual mapping.

## Muscle Tag Conventions

- `primaryMuscles`: muscles expected to receive the main hypertrophy stimulus.
- `secondaryMuscles`: stabilizers or meaningful contributors that should be tracked but not counted as the main target.
- Muscle names are intentionally specific enough to be useful in the UI, but grouped enough for training decisions.

## Workout Categories Added

- Full body: squat, hinge, horizontal push, horizontal pull, vertical push, loaded carry.
- Core: anti-lateral flexion, anti-rotation, trunk flexion/rotation control, overhead shoulder stability.
- Legs: squat, unilateral knee-dominant lunge/split squat, hip hinge, glute bridge, calf/foot/ankle support via carries.
- Upper body: chest, back, shoulders, triceps, biceps/grip, upper-back stabilization.
