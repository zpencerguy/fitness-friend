# EMOM Fitness App Product Steering Document

## Vision

Most fitness apps optimize for exercise libraries, workout randomness, weight loss, or advanced bodybuilding.

This product exists to solve a different problem:

Help normal people build meaningful muscle and strength through simple, sustainable daily workouts.

The target user is not a competitive bodybuilder.

The target user is someone with:

- A job
- A family
- Limited time
- Basic home equipment
- A desire to become visibly stronger and more muscular

The app should feel approachable while quietly applying proven bodybuilding and hypertrophy principles behind the scenes.

## Product Philosophy

### Consistency Beats Optimization

The best workout is the one that gets completed.

A slightly suboptimal workout performed 300 times is more effective than a perfect workout performed 20 times.

Every product decision should increase adherence before increasing complexity.

### Muscle Growth Is the Goal

Many fitness apps optimize for calories burned, sweat generated, or heart rate achieved.

We optimize for:

- Muscle gained
- Strength gained
- Training consistency
- Long-term physical transformation

Users should feel like they are building something, not merely surviving workouts.

### Bodybuilding Principles Work

The fitness industry often overcomplicates muscle growth.

Decades of evidence and real-world results show that most successful physiques are built through:

- Progressive overload
- Sufficient volume
- Recovery
- Protein intake
- Consistency

We borrow heavily from bodybuilding because bodybuilding is fundamentally the study of building muscle.

However, we reject bodybuilding culture that requires:

- Two-hour gym sessions
- Perfect nutrition
- Extreme dieting
- Competitive aspirations

We want bodybuilding results with real-life constraints.

### Structure Beats Randomness

Random workouts feel exciting.

Structured training creates results.

The app should gradually move users from:

> What's today's workout?

to:

> I'm progressing through a training plan.

Users should feel momentum and continuity across weeks.

## Core User

The primary user is a busy adult training at home.

Characteristics:

- 30-50 years old
- Has children and work responsibilities
- Owns dumbbells and/or kettlebells
- Wants visible muscle growth
- Can commit 20-45 minutes daily
- Does not want a complicated program

Examples:

- Software engineers
- Remote workers
- Parents
- Busy professionals

## Product Principles

### EMOM First

The product is not a bodybuilding app.

The product is an EMOM app.

EMOM remains the primary interaction model.

Every feature should ask:

> Can this still be delivered through EMOM?

The challenge is not replacing EMOM.

The challenge is making EMOM smarter.

### Simplicity Over Features

Avoid:

- Endless exercise databases
- Complex periodization systems
- Advanced sports science dashboards

Prefer:

- Clear workouts
- Clear progression
- Clear recommendations

Users should spend more time training than configuring.

### Progressive Overload Must Be Visible

Every workout should answer:

- Am I stronger?
- Am I lifting more?
- Am I doing more reps?
- Am I improving?

Without progression, motivation eventually fades.

The app should continuously surface evidence of growth.

### Recovery Matters

More is not always better.

The app should actively prevent:

- Overtraining
- Excessive volume
- Poor muscle balance

Users should feel recovered enough to continue training tomorrow.

## Training Philosophy

### Muscle Groups Matter

The system should understand:

- Chest
- Back
- Shoulders
- Arms
- Legs
- Core

Training should be balanced across weeks.

No muscle group should be unintentionally neglected.

### Repeat Workouts

Most workout apps over-randomize.

Real muscle growth comes from repeating movements long enough to improve.

The app should encourage:

- Familiar exercises
- Measurable progress
- Planned repetition

A workout should earn its place through results.

### Volume Tracking

The app should understand:

- Direct muscle volume
- Weekly training frequency
- Movement balance

The user should not need to understand training volume.

The system should manage it automatically.

### Home Gym Reality

The app should assume users have:

- Dumbbells
- Kettlebells
- Bodyweight

Everything else is optional.

Programs should be effective without requiring a commercial gym.

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term goal is not simply generating workouts.

The long-term goal is generating transformations.

The ideal outcome is:

A user opens the app every day for a year.

The app quietly manages:

- Exercise selection
- Muscle balance
- Progressive overload
- Recovery
- Long-term planning

At the end of that year, the user is visibly more muscular, stronger, healthier, and more confident.

## Inspiration

We are inspired by the patterns observed in successful long-term muscle-building journeys.

Not transformation challenges.

Not 30-day programs.

Not fitness hacks.

Instead:

- Years of consistent training
- Progressive overload
- Simple equipment
- Sustainable routines
- Measurable growth

The product should feel like a coach guiding a multi-year journey rather than a generator producing today's workout.

## Success Metric

The ultimate success metric is not:

- Workouts generated
- Calories burned
- Minutes trained

The ultimate success metric is:

> Did the user become stronger and more muscular six months from now than they are today?

Every feature should contribute toward that outcome.
